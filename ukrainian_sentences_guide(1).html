Це and Де —
Your First Ukrainian Sentences

Two tiny words unlock your first real sentences. Why Ukrainian drops "is", how intonation changes meaning, and how to connect ideas with і, й, and та.

Це · This is
Де · Where
No verb "to be"
A1 · Week 1

Це — "This is / That is"

Це (tse) means "this is" or "that is". It works the same for all noun genders — no changes needed. Point at something and say Це ___.
Це ▸
Це Марі.
Tse Mari.
This is Marie.
Це ▸
Це Лео.
Tse Leo.
This is Leo.
Це ▸
Це банк.
Tse bank.
This is a bank.
Це ▸
Це ма́ма і та́то.
Tse mama i tato.
This is mum and dad.

Де — "Where?"

Де (de) means "where". Put it at the start of a sentence and raise your voice at the end — that's all it takes to ask a question.
Де? ▸
Де докуме́нти?
De dokumenty?
Where are the documents?
Answer ▸

Tam!
There!

💡 Grammar Insight

Ukrainian drops "is" in the present tense

In English you always need is, are, am — "This is Leo", "We are here". Ukrainian simply omits it in the present tense. The noun or pronoun sits directly next to its complement with no verb in between. Linguists call this a zero copula.

You will see this throughout Week 1. Don't try to add an extra word — the gap where "is" would be is completely normal and correct.

English
This is Leo.
verb "is" required
Ukrainian
Це Лео.
no verb — perfectly correct
Ukrainian
Ма́ма вдо́ма.
Mum (is) at home
Note: In the past and future tenses Ukrainian does use a form of "to be" (був / буде). The zero copula is a present-tense feature only — so what you learn here will need adjusting when you reach those tenses later.
Ukrainian relies heavily on intonation — the rise and fall of your voice — to signal statements versus questions. The words can be identical; only the melody changes.
↘
Statement
Це банк.
Tse bank.
Voice falls at the end. You are stating a fact: "This is a bank."
↗
Yes/No Question
Це банк?
Tse bank?
Voice rises at the end. You are asking: "Is this a bank?"
↗↘
Де? Question
Де документи?
De dokumenty?
Де rises sharply, then the rest of the sentence falls away.
Tip: In natural speech Ukrainians often omit a question mark and rely entirely on intonation. If you hear the voice rise at the end, it's a question — even if it looks like a statement on paper.
Ukrainian has three ways to say "and". They mean the same thing but are chosen based on the surrounding sounds to keep speech flowing smoothly.
WordWhen to use itExamples from Day 1
іi Default "and". Use after a consonant or at the start of a phrase. The most common form. ма́ма і та́то ка́ва і вода́ Марі́ і Лео́
йy After a vowel. Prevents two vowels colliding. Sounds like a quick English "y". ти й він вона́ й він
таta Literary / elevated alternative. Interchangeable with і in most contexts; slightly more formal. хліб та сіль мова та культура
At A1 level, just use і — it works almost everywhere. You'll absorb the й/та distinctions naturally as your listening grows.
