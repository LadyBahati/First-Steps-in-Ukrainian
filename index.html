Six Vowels of Ukrainian
А, Е, І, И, О, У

A pronunciation guide for speakers of European languages

A1 Beginner ~3 min Pronunciation

Why start with vowels?

Mastering Ukrainian starts
with six simple sounds.

If you speak a European language — French, Spanish, English, German, or Italian — you already know these vowels. Let's map them out.

0:20–0:45 А а Vowel 1
А — the open "ah" like "ah"

This is the open 'a' you find in the English Father, the French Papa, or the Spanish and Italian Casa. It's wide, central, and relaxed. Just drop your jaw and say: А.

0:45–1:10 О о Vowel 2
О — the rounded "oh" like "oh"

A rounded, 'open' sound. Think of the English words More or Horse. Your lips should be clearly rounded (forming a small circle) and your tongue is pulled back toward the soft palate.

1:10–1:35 Е е Vowel 3
Е — the short "eh" like "eh"

Don't let the shape fool you. It's not "ee" — it's a mid-open 'e' like the English Pet, the French Bec, the German Bett. It's crisp and flat. Keep it short: Е.

И и Vowels 4–5

І — the bright "ee"

The one with the dot — the smiling vowel. A bright sound like the 'ee' in the English See, the French Vite, or the Italian Sì.

ENGSee
FRVite
ITASì

И — the dull "ih"

The 'black sheep.' It doesn't exist in French. A dull, lower sound — exactly like the 'i' in the English Bit or Sit. Think of German Wissen. Say 'i' but pull your tongue back: И.

ENGBit
GERWissen
Not like the French 'i'. Lower and stiffer.

2:30–2:55 У у Vowel 6
У — the deep "oo" like "oo"

It looks like a 'y' but it sounds like the 'oo' in Boot. This is a deep 'oo' sound, like the French Tout or the Spanish Luna. Push your lips forward like you're about to whistle: У.
